About

Computational tools for social behaviour, cognition, and clinical data.

I work at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience, social interaction, behavioural modelling, and machine learning.

My doctoral research focuses on how people establish shared meaning during interaction, especially in settings where communication cannot rely on familiar words or fixed symbols. I analyse behavioural sequences, trial-wise neural responses, dyadic coordination, and evolving representational structure using reproducible computational pipelines.

This technical profile also supports applied data science: cleaning and modelling noisy human data, building interpretable ML workflows, designing source-grounded RAG tools, and translating clinical or operational questions into testable analyses.

Research / Academia

Shared meaning as a computational problem.

I study communication as an interactional process: people do not simply transmit pre-existing meanings; they jointly build, refine, and stabilize a usable space of possibilities. My work uses behavioural modelling, dual-fMRI, graph methods, and statistical inference to make that process measurable.

01 · Behaviour

Tacit Communication Game

Analysis of an experimental semiotic paradigm where dyads develop novel signals and strategies to coordinate without ordinary language.

02 · Brain

Dual-fMRI

Trial-wise neuroimaging analyses of interacting partners, focused on planning, observation, and the neural structure of communication.

03 · Representation

Representational similarity

Representational similarity analysis across behavioural and neural data to quantify alignment and structure during coordination.

04 · Complexity

Intrinsic dimensionality

Participation-ratio and related analyses for estimating how many effective dimensions organize behavioural and neural state spaces over time.

05 · Structure

Graph and spectral methods

Network analyses of trial-level similarity, Laplacian spectra, null models, and graph structure in emergent communication systems.

06 · Inference

Statistical modelling

Mixed-effects models, GAMs, Bayesian models, permutation testing, FDR/FWER correction, and meta-analytic synthesis for behavioural and neuroimaging data.
